# Dishr — Monetization Ideas (Draft)

Last updated: 2026-04-07

> Early brainstorm — not committed to any of these. Just baseline thinking on what could work without inhibiting users or killing growth.

## Guiding Principles

- Never paywall social features (following, feed, tries, sharing)
- Monetization should feel like a feature, not a tax
- Prioritize revenue streams that also drive growth
- Keep it free-to-use for the core experience
- Subscriptions are off the table — high churn, bad fit for social apps

---

## Idea 1: Affiliate Ingredients — "Shop This Recipe"

Button on recipe detail page that deep-links to Instacart/Amazon Fresh with ingredients pre-loaded into a cart. We earn 3-8% commission on completed orders.

- Zero friction — users see it as a helpful feature
- Free to set up (Instacart Creator program, Amazon Associates)
- Revenue scales directly with recipe views
- ~$2-6 per conversion on avg $50-80 grocery order

**Status:** Not started. Would need Instacart affiliate signup + cart URL builder.

---

## Idea 2: Recipe Card Generator — Share to Social

Auto-generate styled recipe cards (Instagram Story / TikTok format) from any recipe. Free with small "dishr" watermark. $1.99 one-time IAP to remove watermark permanently.

- Every free card shared = free brand exposure
- One-time purchase, not subscription (charged on the spot via Apple/Google IAP)
- ~$1.39 net per purchase after Apple's 30% cut
- Real value is the marketing, paid upgrade is a bonus

**Status:** Not started. Needs card renderer + IAP integration.

---

## Idea 3: Brand Partnerships

Food brands pay to feature recipes using their products. Native content, not display ads. "Featured by [Brand]" badge on curated recipes.

- $500-2K per campaign depending on reach
- Only viable at 5K+ MAU with engagement data to pitch
- Could also do "sponsored try" campaigns

**Status:** Future — needs user base first.

---

## Other Ideas to Explore

- Tipping / creator support ("buy me a coffee" on popular recipes)
- Premium cook mode features (timers, voice, serving scaler) as one-time IAP
- Data/trends licensing to food brands (what recipes are trending)
- Recipe collections as a paid feature
- Print-quality recipe book generation (annual, custom)
- Local restaurant/meal kit partnerships

---

## What We're NOT Doing

- Subscriptions
- Display ads
- Paywalling imports or social features
- Anything that adds friction to the core loop

---

## Rough Implementation Priority

1. Recipe Card Generator (growth engine + revenue)
2. Affiliate "Shop Ingredients" (passive income)
3. Brand partnerships (manual outreach, no code)
4. Everything else TBD based on what users actually want
